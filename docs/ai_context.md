:notebook_with_decorative_cover: General info

What is Hackanomous?

Hackanomous (#hackanomous , https://hackanomous.vercel.app/) is a Hack Club YSWS where you build your AI skills, make something awesome to show off to your family and friends, and win some AI credits, and even more along the way. 
Submit any AI or ML related project at all (subject to our T&Cs) and get a pile of stickers, credits or just make something awesome and win big.
Sounds Fun? Join Now!

How long will Hackanomous run for?

Hackanomous' end date is to be confirmed, but was can assure you that it will be at least three months! This is subject to change.


:timepheus_clk:  Time tracking

How do I track my time spent coding / designing?

Tracking your time spent on coding or designing your projects is very important to how your project reviews go and more. 

You will have to track your time using Hackatime, which a free and open source software for Hack Clubbers to use. It will measures the amount of time you spend on a project. See #channel for more info. 

What if my editor isn't supported? You can use Lapse, which is part of Hackatime, but please check around before using.


Submitting your Projects and Getting Bolts



How do I submit my project?

Submitting your project shares it with our amazing reviewers! Our reviewers will then review your project and give you some feedback and trigger your bolts payout! 

When will I get my bolts?

You will receive your bolts if your project gets approved. Based off the quality of the project and how much you work on it, you will receive a certain amount of bolts! 


What can I spend my bolts on? 

You can spend bolts in two places. 
The first is funding for your hardware projects! When you create a hardware project, you will get the ability to spend some of your bolts on funding / grants to help you build your hardware projects.
The other is the shop items where you can receive AI credits, piles of stickers and more fun items! However, if you save your bolts you can WIN BIG! 
If you have any questions on the shop or on unfair bolt payouts, please send a message in the #channel channel!

:scroll: Rules and restrictions

Who's able to take part?

Like other Hack Club events, Hackanomous is open to teenagers aged 13–18 years old.
Unfortunately, since we get a lot of adults trying to sneak in, we'll need to verify your age using student or Government ID before shipping you prizes. This is part of Hack Club, so please ask in other channels about identity verification and other eligibility for YSWS questions! 

What kinds of projects can I make?

Any AI related projects! You can submit any kind of AI or ML related project! It can either be hardware or software or anything related! 
Please remember that this doesn’t mean you are allowed to Vibe Code. Your projects must be created by you, and all AI usage declared and only used to aid your programming or designing! 
Furthermore, hardware and software projects each respectively have different ways to grade / review due to difference in timings / effort required! Again, if you have any questions, please ask in #channel 


Can I also submit my project to other Hack Club programs?

Hack Club runs other You Ship, We Ship programs throughout the year. But Hackanomous does not allow double-dipping: you can't submit your Hackanomous projects to any other Hack Club programs that give you a reward.


Can I collaborate with other people? (e.g. team projects)

Yes! You can work on projects in a group/pair of people up to 5 people as a maximum. If you're all submitting to Hackanomous, follow these guidelines:

* Each contributor must each have separate commits and Hackatime hours based upon what each person did. This must also be explained in descriptions etc! 
* Each contributor should make a Hackanomous project under their respective account
    * Ensure you mention that you are in a group and state who else is in the group. If you wish, you can also say what each other person did! 

Can I use AI? 

You can use AI tools to help you code or design, as long as the project contains your own creative input (no vibe coding) and you declare how you've used AI clearly.
You can use AI to aid your programming or designing as long as you're transparent about your use of it in descriptions, etc! 
Please don't submit projects fully made by AI tools - we want to see people learning how AI works and designing their projects mainly themselves. 
Projects have a maximum of 30% AI Usage! 


:package: Orders

Will I need to pay shipping costs?

No, Hack Club pays to ship prizes internationally.

Will I need to pay customs fees?

This will depend based on each item! Please check in the shop or again, send a message in our help channel! 


How long do items take to arrive?

Items can take up to a month to arrive. It can depend on the item or where it is being shipped from. Please ask for help in our help channel for more info! 


Can I track my order?

It depends on what you ordered.
 • Stickers and other mail items are sent via regular postal mail and do not have tracking.
 • For other, generally bigger items, you will receive tracking through whichever service is used. If you can’t find your tracking number / doesn’t work, please ask in our help channel! 


:raising_hand: Helping out behind-the-scenes

How can I help Hackanomous?

Aside from making great projects?
Spread the word! Tell others either in person or on slack to join! 

Can I help others in #channel?

Yes, please do! Just follow the golden rule: read the FAQ (as it's the source of truth), and read it again before answering someone else's question! 
Tip: if you're unsure of a fact, it's best to check or not say anything to avoid giving incorrect advice. You can of course give subjective advice and tips but clearly label it as such so that nobody's confused.
You can also tag either: @user, @user or @user (the Organisers) ONLY if you either you or the person has been waiting longer than 24 hours for a solution in ONLY #channel! 

How do I join the support team?

Just help people out in #channel ! If you're helping people, you will be noticed and very likely asked if you'd like to join the support team as a helper! Helper rewards are to be worked out - but obviously helping is nice anyways! 

I found a bug!

It's best to report it in #channel, and the support team will triage it and make an issue if there isn't one already.
Alternatively, if you've identified a bug and can provide steps to reproduce it, expected/actual behaviour, and screenshot(s), you can make your message more detailed and tag the organisers.
PS: We will try to get you a grant for your report! 

Can I ping the Organisers?

Yes, but only if you have a bug to report OR you have been waiting longer than 24 hours. Please remember that our organisers and reviewers also have busy lives and based on holidays and timezones - therefore things may be delayed! 

Can I join the Fraud Team?

No, you cannot. Due to its nature, the Fraud squad is an invite-only team of people who we have to give very high levels of trust.
